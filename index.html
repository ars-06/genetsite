Hi!

Genet Elementary School is continuing a 81-year tradition of excellence that began with the opening of what was the first East Greenbush Central School District building in September 1940. On the heels of the Great Depression, the East Greenbush community pledged to build a state-of-the-art building to house students in kindergarten through 12th grade. The Genet faculty and staff are committed to developing and expanding quality programs for our children and to enhance that rich tradition of quality education for all.
Our 430 students, in grades K-5, begin each day by passing through the lobby of our magnificent building, greeted by staff, and ready to absorb all that our school has to offer. Numerous activities and special programs are offered throughout the year to enhance our young learners’ experiences at school. The Fall Fun Run, Halloween celebrations, Morning Character Education Assemblies, Concerts, the STEM Fair, Student Council Food Drives, school-wide picnics, live theater performances, and after-school “Explore and Grow” enrichment classes are but a few of the fun activities that make Genet such a special learning environment.

School Achievements

Sumdog Inter-School Mathimatics Compition
Genet and Bell Top Students Excel in Online Math Competition

Fifth graders from Genet and Bell Top Elementary Schools recently participated in Sumdog’s online math contest for Columbia, Rensselaer and Greene counties. Students answered math questions at their grade level as part of online games during the week-long voluntary competition. Congratulations to Mrs. Murawski’s class from Genet for winning the competition.
